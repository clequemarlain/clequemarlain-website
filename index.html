Welcome to my website

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Avignon University, within the Laboratoire Informatique d’Avignon (LIA, EA 4128). My PhD is part of the EU–Japan project CoGNETs, where I contribute to work package WP3 on pricing and bidding algorithms for distributed edge computing systems.

Broadly, my research lies at the intersection of game theory, online learning and edge/fog computing. I study repeated resource allocation games, with a special focus on the Kelly mechanism, budget constraints, and α-fair utilities. I am interested in the existence and uniqueness of equilibria, and in the convergence properties of learning dynamics such as synchronous best-response, Online Gradient Descent and Dual Averaging.

News

In 2025–2026, I am preparing a paper on best-response learning in budgeted α-fair Kelly mechanisms (accepted for NetGCoop 2025) and working on an extended journal version. I am also involved in the preparation of WP3 deliverables for CoGNETs and in teaching activities at Avignon University.

Short bio

Before starting my PhD, I obtained a Master’s degree “Algorithmics, Symbolic Computation and Numerical Optimization for AI” with honours from the University of Limoges, and a Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics (2nd of my cohort, honours). During my Master’s internship at Clecim, I worked on deep learning models (autoencoders) for image classification and reconstruction with very limited labelled data.
